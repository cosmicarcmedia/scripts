01
The Bit — Spill the Beans / Tea
Table, one prop per script. One metaphor, one message, walk off.
4 scripts
SHOOT ORDER: Film all four bits back to back first — [spill the beans], [spill the tea], [blow the whistle], [let the cat out of the bag] — one take each with props ready. Then reset and film all the talking sections. Far more efficient than doing each script start to finish.
1.1 Your Trash Bill Shouldn't Look Like This (PROP: BEANS)
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
I've been wanting to say this for a while. Your trash bill should not look like a politician's stock portfolio.
2
You call to sign up, they quote you $24 a month. Sounds reasonable. Then the bill comes.
3
There's a line for the base service. A line for the fuel surcharge. Environmental recovery fee. Administrative charge. Sales tax on all of it. That $24 is now $54.
4 — cue
[spill the beans] There it is.
5
We quote you one number. That's the total. Tax included. No surcharges. No extra fees. The number we tell you is the number you pay. Go to our website right now. All there.
6
If we ever raise the price, you get a full month's notice and a reason why. Three times in three years. That's it. [walk off]
1.2 The Fine Print That Costs You (PROP: TEA)
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1 — cue
[spill the tea] Here's something most trash companies would rather you didn't know.
2
If you signed up with a major company in the last few years, there's a decent chance you're in a multi-year contract. It was in the fine print.
3
Which makes sense — it was designed to be skimmed. If you want out early, you owe them. Sometimes hundreds of dollars.
4
I know because I ran their dumpster at my own house. It cost me over a thousand dollars to break that contract. To cancel a service I was already overpaying for.
5
Our service agreement is one page. Says "non-binding" right at the top. You want to leave? Leave. No buyout. No collections.
6
Because if I have to lock you in to keep you, I'm not earning you. I'm just keeping you from leaving. Those are not the same thing.
1.3 The $24 That Isn't $24 (PROP: WHISTLE)
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
Someone needs to call this out. So I will.
2
Most big companies advertise a low number. Let's say $24 a month. That's the base rate.
3
Then: fuel surcharge. Extra. Environmental recovery fee. Extra. Administrative charge. Extra. Sales tax calculated on all of the above.
4
By the time that hits your bill, you're looking at $50, $54, sometimes $60. And you signed a contract at the $24 price without knowing what was coming. That's not an oversight. That's the model.
5 — cue
[blow the whistle]
6
Our price includes everything. Service, fuel, truck, driver. Sales tax. One number. If it ever changes, you'll know a month before it happens. And you'll know why.
1.4 Before You Sign Up With Anyone (PROP: CAT)
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
Before you sign up with any trash company — not just us, anyone — try something first.
2
Google how to cancel their service. Not how to sign up. How to cancel. See how easy that information is to find.
3 — cue
[let the cat out of the bag]
4
If cancellation info is buried, that's not an oversight. That's a design decision. Companies that make it easy to leave don't need to hide it.
5
We don't have a cancellation page because there's nothing to cancel. You call us. We pick up the cart.
6
No fee. No notice period. No arguing with anyone on the phone. You just stop. Try finding that offer from the other guys.
02
Origin Story
One longer video. Outside or by the truck. This has never been on camera.
1 script
Six movements through the story. Each one is a cut to a new position or angle.
2.1 Why a Trooper and a Teacher Started a Trash Company
~2–3 min6 cuts
1 — standing, neutral
Six years as a Texas State Trooper. My wife was a schoolteacher for five. And then we started a trash company. I know. It's a pivot.
2 — wider shot
We wanted to build something we owned. Something that served the community we already lived in. When we looked at trash service in this area — the contracts, the fees, the companies that weren't from here — we thought we could do it better.
3 — by the truck
So we made a short list of things we would not do. No multi-year contracts. No hidden fees. One price that includes everything.
4 — tighter angle
And when we mess up — and we do — we fix it all the way. Money back. Another pickup. And a $10 credit on your next bill. An apology should mean something or it doesn't.
5 — walking
Three years later: 2,400 households. Fifteen communities. Three price increases, each with a full month's notice. One customer lost on the last one. I'll take that trade every time.
6 — back to neutral
I moved here when I was 18. This is home. I see these people at church, at football games, at the bank. I wear this hat everywhere. You're not calling a 1-800 number. You're calling someone who drove your road last week.
03
Straight Talk
Direct to camera. No props, no truck. Six positions per script.
4 scripts
Step, angle, or frame change between every numbered spot. Even small moves — step left, lean forward, slightly closer — register as cuts when edited.
3.1 The Total Price Is the Total Price
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
When you ask us what it costs, we tell you the total. Not the base rate. The total. Tax included.
2
That sounds like it should be normal. It isn't.
3
Most companies quote a low number then add fuel surcharges, environmental fees, administrative charges. By the time the bill arrives, it's 30% more.
4
The number you signed up for was a teaser. The real price came later. That's not an accident. That's the model.
5
We've had three price increases in three years. Every single one with a full month's notice and a real explanation.
6
Not a form letter. An actual reason. Because if you're going to charge people more, tell them why and give them time to decide. That's the whole policy.
3.2 What Happens When We Miss Your Pickup
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
We miss pickups sometimes. West Texas roads, equipment, weather. It happens.
2
Here's our policy when it does. You get your money back for that period.
3
We either come back the next day, or you put extra out the following week at no charge.
4
And on top of that — a $10 credit on your next bill. About a third of your monthly payment. Off.
5
Not instead of fixing it. In addition to it.
6
A lot of our best reviews came from customers we let down first. That's not luck. That's the policy working.
3.3 Why We Don't Do Contracts
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
You should be free to choose who picks up your trash the same way you choose anything else. Change your mind? Go somewhere else.
2
That should just be how it works.
3
The reason we don't do contracts isn't a strategy. It's a belief. If I have to lock you in to keep you, I'm not earning you.
4
I'm just keeping you from leaving. Those are not the same thing. I know because I've been a customer of a company that cost me a thousand dollars to leave.
5
I'm not building that company.
6
Our agreement is one page. Non-binding at the top. Sign up online in three minutes. We want to win your business every month. Not hold it. Tell us if we're not.
3.4 What You're Actually Paying For
~60–90 sec6 cuts
1
When you pay for trash service, you're paying for one thing. A cart shows up, gets emptied, disappears. Same day. Every week.
2
That's the service.
3
You're not paying a fuel surcharge. You're not paying an environmental recovery fee. Those are things companies add after you've already agreed to the price.
4
They're not part of the service. They're additional revenue wrapped in language that sounds necessary.
5
None of it is.
6
We charge one number. Cart, driver, fuel, truck, everything. Sales tax. That's it. Go to our website. Price is right there. No forms. No waiting.

Fun & Visual

4.1 Anatomy of a Trash Truck
~90–120 sec6 cuts — walk around the truck
1 — front
Most people see this thing come by their house every week and think: big, loud, early. Fair. But there's a lot going on here.
2 — cab
Up here is the cab. Driver's office. Eight-hour day minimum. Route sheet, radio, a lot of coffee. The seat has seen better decades.
3 — compaction unit
Down here is the compaction unit. Runs on hydraulics. Blade pressure around a thousand PSI.
4 — explaining
That takes your week's worth of garbage and compresses it to about a third of its original size. Every couple of pickups, it cycles automatically.
5 — back of truck
That's the sound you hear when we drive away from your house. Not just leaving — making room for the next stop.
6 — loading section
Back here is the loading section. Forks hook under the lip of the cart. Arm lifts it, tips it, dumps it in. By end of route, we're carrying five to eight tons. Every day.
